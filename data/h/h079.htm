Headstone, gothic top. Almost identical to H78. Decorated top centre by a raised cross on a plinth. This is enclosed by a hood-shaped raised bead, with flowers having 9 long petals either side. The first line of the inscription circles around the cross. The bottom part of the stone has spalled. The inscription faces west.

In affectionate remembrance of
Sarah,
the wife of
John FAULKS,
who fell asleep in Jesus
April 12 1871,
aged 59 years.
To the great grief of her
beloved family.
For to me is Christ and
to die is gain
having a desire to depart and to
be w[ith] Christ which[h] [is] far [better.]
Ph[i---- ] 23.

[BR: "1871 17 Apr FAULKS Sarah 59yrs North Leigh". The inscription is based on Philippians 1:21-23. See notes on H78. ]

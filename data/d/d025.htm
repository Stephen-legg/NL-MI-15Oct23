Cope stone - large, 2-tiered, on plinth. Iron railings, broken but complete. Single twisted wrought iron bar railings, decorated each end with a trefoil. Corner posts are also twisted wrought iron. One is topped with a finial formed from an outline of four slender edges of a bell shape, others have been broken off. On the west end of the cope stone there is an intricately carved stone wreath, in high relief, featuring five petalled blooms on a circular foliage wreath. Inscription on the south side :

In loving memory of
John JOSLIN.
Who died April 8th. 1896, aged 69 years.
Thou knowest not what a day may bring forth. Prov. 2[7]th. chap. 1st. verse.

Inscription on the north side :

In affectionate memory of
Mary JOSLIN, widow of John JOSLIN,
who died September 29th. 1909, aged 75 years.
"I will give you rest.".

Obelisk on two-tier plinth. Coped body stone. There is no inscription on the obelisk, but there is an IHS inscription in gothic lettering within a roundel low down on the east face. This is one of two identical obelisks standing side by side - see also E7. The inscription on the south face of the coped body stone reads as follows:

In remembrance of
Henry McKINLAY.
Gardener at Eynsham Hall
who died April 3rd 1875
aged 39 years
He was much respected by his employer James Mason who erected this stone.

[BR - "1875 7 Apr McKINLEY Henry 40yrs Eynsham Hall". 1871 census, unmarried, gardener at Eynsham Hall, b. 1837.]

Mantel top chest tomb. Grade II listed - "SP3813 21/167- Chest tomb approx. 8m S of S porch of Church of St. Mary. Early C19. Limestone. Moulded plinth and cornice; corner balusters. Commemorates Sarah Hutt, d.1819, and others". The monument is constructed from a reddish "Hornton" type of limestone, unusual in North Leigh. The remains of railings around the base. There are two associated ledger stones, one to the north and one to the south of the tomb. There are two inscriptions on the south and north sides, and one on each of the east and west sides. South elevation, left side inscription:

In memory of
Sarah, the wife of
John HUTT,
who departed this life
July the 6th 1819,
aged 88 years.

South elevation, right side inscription:

To the memory of
Sarah the wife of
Josiah JONES
who departed this life
September 2nd 1834
aged 70 years

West elevation inscription:

To the memory
of
John HUTT
who died
June 10th
1838
Aged 85 years

North elevation, left side inscription:

In memory of
John HUTT who
departed this life
Febry. ye 17, 1800. Aged 77 years
[----] wife and children all agree
Prepare yourselves to follow me
[To] [mansions] of eternal rest
To be with Christ for ever blest

North elevation, right side inscription:

Also
three sons by the name of
William, two daughters
by the name of Elizabeth
Mary and Alice, sons
and daughters of John and
Sarah HUTT, who all
died in their infancy.

East elevation inscription:

To the memory
of
Henry HUTT
who died
Janry. 29th.
1840.
Aged [8]4 years
[Judge] not that he [---]
[judged] VII Chap Matt [I]

Ledger stone, south side - the initials (S.H. - Sarah Hutt) are eroded away, leaving the date:

1819

Ledger stone, north side, to Henry Hutt:

H. H.

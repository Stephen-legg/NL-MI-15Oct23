Headstone - cross top, in white marble. There is possibly a very faint inscription across the horizontal portion of the cross, but it is now completely illegible. The main inscription is on the lower part of the headstone under the cross top.

George BROWN aged [1] year
10 months
Eleanora [Ma--] Fanny [BROWN]
aged [5] years
George BROWN aged 11 [months]
infant children of [John & ]
Fanny BROWN

[BR -" 1845 2 Apr BROWN George 14mths North Leigh"; Baptism: "1844 25 Feb BROWN George; John & Frances; Farmer; North Leigh" -- AND -- BR: "1858 27 Aug BROWN Eleanor Mary Fanny 5yrs Neat Enstone" -- AND -- "1857 13 Apr BROWN George 11mths Neat Enstone" . In the 1851 census John Brown is aged 40 and was born at Wilcote. He is living in Neat Enstone with wife Fanny aged 37, born Witney. He is a farmer employing 17 labourers and lives with his son John aged 14 and six daughters aged from 13 down to 1 month, and 5 servants. In the 1841 census John & Fanny occupied Bridewell Farm, North Leigh.]
